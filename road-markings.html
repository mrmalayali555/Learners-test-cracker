Road Markings Explained — Kerala Learners Test Guide

Understanding road markings is essential for safe driving and acing your Kerala learners licence test. This guide explains common markings you'll encounter on Kerala's roads, including yellow and white lines, double lines, zebra crossings, and more.

Yellow Line

A solid yellow line marks the center of the road where overtaking is prohibited on both sides. A broken yellow line indicates that vehicles may cross the line to overtake when safe. Always follow yellow lines to prevent head-on collisions, especially on Kerala's two-lane highways.

White Line

White lines separate traffic moving in the same direction. A solid white line at the road edge marks the boundary of the carriageway, while broken white lines divide lanes. Edge lines help drivers stay within their lane, particularly at night or in poor visibility.

Double Line

Double yellow or white lines indicate a no-overtaking zone for all vehicles. These are typically found on roads with limited visibility, sharp curves, or high-speed traffic. Crossing double lines is strictly prohibited under the Motor Vehicles Act.

Zebra Crossing

Identified by alternating black and white stripes, zebra crossings allow pedestrians to cross safely. Drivers must slow down and yield to pedestrians on zebra crossings. Unmarked crossings may exist at intersections, but marked ones are clearly visible.

Stop Line

A thick white line ahead of a zebra crossing or intersection is the stop line. Vehicles must halt before this line at red signals or stop signs. Stop lines ensure orderly movement and prevent congestion at junctions.

Edge Line

Solid white lines mark the outer edge of the road, guiding drivers to stay within the carriageway. On multi-lane roads, edge lines separate traffic from shoulders or barriers. They are crucial for maintaining lane discipline, especially on curved sections.

Frequently asked questions

Can I cross a solid yellow line to overtake?

No, overtaking across solid yellow lines is illegal and dangerous under Kerala traffic rules.

What does a broken white line mean?

It allows vehicles to change lanes when safe, but does not permit crossing into opposing traffic.

Are zebra crossings only at intersections?

No, zebra crossings can exist at mid-block locations where pedestrian crossings are needed, not just at junctions.

What happens if I stop before the stop line?

Stopping before the stop line is a traffic violation that may attract a fine as per the Motor Vehicles Act.
