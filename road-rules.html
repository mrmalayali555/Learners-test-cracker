Road Rules for the Kerala Learners Test

Preparing for your Kerala learners licence test requires a strong grasp of road rules, safe driving practices, and traffic regulations specific to Indian roads. Mastering these fundamentals ensures road safety for you and others while you learn to drive under supervision.

Overtaking Rules

Overtaking must be done with caution and strictly when safe. Always check mirrors and blind spots before moving into the opposite lane. Use hand signals if indicators are not functional. In India you overtake on the right side of the vehicle ahead. Passing on the left is only allowed when the vehicle in front is signalling or turning right, or in slow multi-lane traffic. Never overtake near school zones, sharp curves, junctions or pedestrian crossings. The Motor Vehicles Act requires that overtaking must never endanger other road users, and you must not overtake where a sign or road marking prohibits it.

Parking Rules

Parking must be done parallel to the direction of traffic flow in designated areas. Always use indicators and check for oncoming traffic before reversing. Never park on footpaths, cycle lanes, zebra crossings, junctions, or in front of entrances, and always stop before the stop line at crossings. In Kerala, parking near bus stops or railway crossings is prohibited as per local traffic regulations.

Speed Limits

Speed limits vary by road type and vehicle category. Limits differ for cars, motorcycles, and heavy vehicles, and between towns, highways and expressways, so there is no single number to memorise. Always follow the speed shown on the posted sign for that road, and treat it as the maximum, not a target. Exceeding the limit is an offence under the Motor Vehicles Act. Check the current Kerala MVD / Parivahan notifications for exact category limits.

Lane Discipline

Maintain your lane and avoid sudden lane changes. Drive in the leftmost lane unless overtaking. Keep a safe distance from vehicles ahead—use the two-second rule (maintain a gap of two vehicle lengths). Unauthorized lane switching, especially across solid white lines, is illegal and increases accident risk. Refer to the Kerala MVD’s lane discipline guidelines on the Parivahan Sarathi portal.

One-Way and Right of Way

One-way streets are marked with signs; always follow directional arrows. At intersections, yield to vehicles already in the junction. Pedestrians have right of way at zebra crossings. At roundabouts, vehicles already circulating have priority. Never turn right on a red light unless a sign explicitly permits it. Right of way rules are standardized under the Motor Vehicles Act.

Keep Left

All vehicles must keep to the left side of the road. This applies to two-wheelers, cars, and buses. On multi-lane roads, stay in the left lane except when overtaking. Driving on the right side is illegal and dangerous. The "keep left" rule reduces head-on collisions and ensures smooth traffic flow as per Kerala traffic norms.

Frequently asked questions

Question?

Can I drive a car with a learners licence?

Yes, but only while accompanied by a person holding a valid driving licence for that class of vehicle, seated so as to supervise you, and with an 'L' (learner) plate displayed.

Question?

What happens if I drive without L-plates?

Driving without proper L-plates is an offense under the Motor Vehicles Act and may attract penalties as per Kerala MVD rules.

Question?

Are L-licence holders allowed on highways?

Yes, learners with a valid L-licence may use highways under supervision of a licensed driver, following all traffic rules.

Question?

How long is a learners licence valid?

A learners licence is valid for 6 months from the date of issuance, as per the current Motor Vehicles Act / Kerala MVD schedule (verify on the Parivahan portal).
